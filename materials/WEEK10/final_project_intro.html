Overview

The Final Project is your opportunity to design and code your own original Python program that demonstrates the full range of skills you’ve developed this semester. You’ll choose your own topic, design it using pseudocode in Week 12, and then turn that plan into a working program by Week 15. This is your chance to be creative, as long as your program uses key programming structures and a dictionary to organize data.

Project Goals

  • Plan, design, and implement a complete Python program from scratch.
  • Demonstrate use of variables, loops, conditionals, and functions.
  • Store and manage information in a dictionary (required).
  • Include user input, calculations or data processing, and output.
  • Show clear organization and logical problem-solving in your pseudocode and code.
  • Be able to communicate plans and reflections to non technical stakeholders.

Requirements

  • Dictionary: Your program must use at least one dictionary to store and retrieve data (for example, a menu of items, student records, quiz questions, or travel destinations).
  • Loops: Use a for or while loop for repetition (input, validation, or calculations).
  • Functions: Include at least one custom function to organize your code.
  • Error Handling: Include basic input validation or try/except blocks for robustness.
  • Reflection: Write a short paragraph explaining your process, challenges, and what you learned.

Reflection

Summary and reflections about your learning experience.

Choosing a Topic

You will select your own topic. It should solve a small, clear problem or provide useful or fun interaction. The topic should fit within your skills and time frame, but it’s open-ended to encourage creativity.

Timeline

  • Week 12: Submit your pseudocode design (this is your project plan).
  • Weeks 13–14: Build your Python program based on the pseudocode. Test and debug regularly.
  • Week 15: Submit final project and reflection by Sunday December 14th, 11:59 PM.
